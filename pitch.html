REQUISITION 01

Three Things This Game
Is Actually Built On

No single inspiration explains JunkYardMechs. It's a collision of three — and none of them get watered down to fit the others.

CERTIFIED
// 01

Arm-Swing Locomotion

No teleporting, no smooth-stick gliding. You haul your hull forward by swinging your arms, vault over wreckage, and launch off ledges with your own momentum — the same physical movement Gorilla Tag built its name on, now strapped to forty feet of scrap metal.

// 02

Large-Scale Combat

Missions don't stay small. Objectives stack, reinforcements drop in mid-fight, and the battlefield fills with mechs, drones, and chaos well beyond what your five-person squad alone could hold the line against — Helldivers-scale stakes, mech-scale collateral damage.

// 03

Cyberpunk Junkyard

Neon signage bleeds through smog over rust and rebar. Every mech is visibly welded together from salvage, chop-shop wiring, and stolen corpo tech — a world that looks expensive to live in and cheap to die in.

REQUISITION 02

The Yard

Before any mission, you land in the social lobby — a shared garage where up to ten players wrench on their mechs, swap parts, and size each other up. No menus standing between you and your crew. If your loadout isn't ready, this is where you fix it.

Lobby capacity10 players
PurposeCustomize / Meet / Idle
PressureNone — deploy when ready
